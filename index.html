Highest Poverty Rates in the United States

By Juliet La Sala

The top ten states with the highest poverty rates are New Mexico, Mississippi, Louisiana, Arkansas, Kentucly, Oklahoma, West Virginia, Alabama, District of Columbia, and Texas.

The question as to why these particular states suffer from the highest poverty rate in the country is broad and multifaceted. Only two out of the ten states are considered "blue" states (D.C. and New Mexico) while the other eight are considered "red." Despite living at or below the poevrty line, voters in these states tend to vote for representatives that cut government assistance programs and lower taxes for those at the top of the economic bracket. Because these states have a lower cost of living, daily necessities tend to be less expensive. However differences in political and philosophical ideologies tend to be the driving force behind voter turnout. Al Cross, director of the Institute for Rural Journalism and Community Issues at the University of Kentucky noted that regional poverty contributes to voter turnout "born of isolation, geographic and political; exploitation, of timber and coal; and poor education."

Data source: United States Census Bureau November 9, 2023
